Databases store a variety of data types to meet the needs of different kinds of information and applications.
The choice of which data types to use in a database depends on the nature of the data that will be stored and processed.
Here are some of the most common data types in a database:

1 - Text (String):

CHAR: Stores fixed-length strings. Used when values have a constant length.
VARCHAR: Stores variable-length strings. Appropriate for values with varying lengths.
TEXT: Stores very long strings, such as documents or descriptions.

2 - Numeric:

INTEGER (INT): Stores whole numbers.
FLOAT: Stores floating-point numbers, commonly used for values with decimal places.
NUMERIC (DECIMAL): Stores numbers with a specific precision, commonly used in financial applications.

3 - Date and Time:

DATE: Stores dates without time information.
TIME: Stores time information.
TIMESTAMP: Combines date and time in a single type.

4 - Boolean:

BOOLEAN (BOOL): Stores true or false values.

5 - Binary:

BLOB (Binary Large Object): Stores binary data, such as images, videos, or files.
BIT: Stores binary values, such as 0 or 1.

These are some of the common data types in relational databases. However,
the choice of data types may vary depending on the database management system (DBMS) and the specific needs of an application.
In addition, some DBMSs also offer custom data types that can be tailored to specific business requirements.
